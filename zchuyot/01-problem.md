# 01 — The problem: the מיצוי זכויות gap

## What "מיצוי זכויות" means operationally

Israel's welfare/rights system is **claim-based**: almost nothing is granted
automatically. Entitlement exists on paper the moment your life state matches a
rule (disability, unemployment, birth, bereavement, age, low income, reserve
duty, chronic illness…), but money/services flow only after you (a) know the
right exists, (b) prove you match, (c) file the correct claim with the correct
body within the correct window.

מיצוי זכויות = closing the distance between *entitled* and *received*.

## Why the gap exists (failure modes)

1. **Awareness** — people don't know the right exists. Rights are scattered
   across ביטוח לאומי, מס הכנסה, משרד הרווחה, משרד הבריאות, קופות חולים, משרד
   השיכון, רשויות מקומיות, קרנות פרטיות. Nobody has the full map in their head.
2. **Language** — the rules are written in legal-bureaucratic Hebrew. Weakest
   populations include Arabic speakers, Russian speakers, Haredim with limited
   internet access, olim.
3. **State-blindness** — the person doesn't recognize *their own situation* as
   the trigger. A woman caring for a sick parent doesn't search "גמלת סיעוד";
   she's "just helping אמא". The bureaucratic category and the lived experience
   don't share vocabulary. **This is the core insight the AI thesis attacks.**
4. **Process friction** — forms, medical committees, queues, documents,
   deadlines, appeals. Each step sheds a percentage of claimants.
5. **Crisis timing** — rights trigger exactly when capacity is lowest: after a
   death, a diagnosis, a layoff, a birth, a war. The system demands maximal
   bureaucratic competence at the moment of minimal personal bandwidth.
6. **Distrust / shame** — some populations assume rejection, or feel claiming
   is begging.

## Who has the biggest gap

- Elderly (סיעוד, השלמת הכנסה, הנחות ארנונה, ניצולי שואה)
- People with disabilities / chronic illness (נכות כללית, ניידות, פטור ממס)
- Caregivers of the above (the invisible category)
- Low-income workers (מענק עבודה / mas hachnasa shlili — chronically
  under-claimed; the מענק עבודה is the textbook example of a right with
  massive known under-take-up)
- Bereaved / widows / orphans
- Olim, Arabic-speaking citizens, Haredi society
- Post-trauma & miluim populations (post-2023 this exploded: הלומי קרב,
  משפחות מפונים, נפגעי פעולות איבה — a politically live and well-funded area)
- Anyone owed a tax refund (salaried workers who changed jobs, worked partial
  years, had kids — the commercial players prove the demand)

## The one-line problem statement

> The state speaks in categories; people live in stories. Whoever translates
> story → categories → executed claims, at scale and near-zero cost, closes
> the gap.
